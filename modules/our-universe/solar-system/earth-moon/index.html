The Moon is moving around Earth. One full trip is called an orbit.

Earth's gravity pulls on the Moon. The Moon keeps moving forward, so it goes around instead of falling straight down.

The real Moon takes about 27 days to go around Earth once. This lab speeds it up so we can watch it.

Yes. Earth wobbles a tiny bit because the Moon pulls back. Space is full of teamwork.

This is called tidal locking. The Moon spins once during each orbit, so the same near side keeps facing Earth.

Earth and the Moon both move around their shared balance point. That point is called the barycenter.

Earth spins about 28 times during one Moon orbit. Use Slow, Normal, and Fast to see the spin and orbit change together.
